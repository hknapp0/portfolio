Projects

Non-profit Homepage Redesign

My groupmate and I redesigned the YMHProject.org navigation and homepage. The original YMH Project website wasn’t user friendly, especially for young adults in crisis. We wanted to focus on minimizing unnecessary information, making the most valuable information quickly accessible and visible resulting in a more pleasant, productive experience for our users. We also discovered emphasizing the most necessary information in the navigation and home screen will accomplish a more beneficial, useful experience for our users.

View YMHProject.com Redesign

Governemnt Agency Homepage Redesign

My groupmates and I redesigned the USDA.gov homepage to make it more accessible. We found that the original USDA website was over crowded and hard for users to understand. After redesigning the USDA homepage, we found that users were better able to understand the content and find what they were looking for faster. We also learned that simplifying the content made it easier for users to navigate and absorb the content.

View USDA.gov Redesign

On Queue

My groupmates and I created an app called “On Queue” that allowed you to live track line wait times. Not only could you view what other people posted, you could create your own post to earn points that allowed you to earn a “skip the line” pass. Great for people who are always late, have fomo, and are frequent night lifers. Also allows people with anxiety to have peace of mind, and in case of bad weather keeps people safe.
